Fajita Cheeseburger

Description

This fajita cheeseburger is a delicious twist on a classic burger. It's made with seasoned ground beef, sautéed peppers and onions, and melted cheese, all served on a toasted bun. It's a flavorful and satisfying meal that's perfect for a summer cookout or weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 4 slices pepper jack cheese
  • 4 hamburger buns, toasted

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well and form into 4 patties.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add patties and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  3. While the patties are cooking, heat another skillet over medium heat. Add sliced bell peppers and onion and sauté for 5-7 minutes, or until softened.
  4. Top each burger patty with a slice of pepper jack cheese and cook for an additional minute, or until cheese is melted.
  5. Place each patty on a toasted bun and top with sautéed peppers and onions. Serve hot.
